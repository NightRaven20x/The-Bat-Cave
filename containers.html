Understanding Containers (Div vs Span)

The Block-Level DIV

A <div> is a block-level container. It takes up the entire width of the page by default, pushing other elements to the next line. Think of it like a secure containment cell in Arkham Asylum.

The Inline SPAN

A <span> is an inline container. It only takes up as much width as necessary, allowing it to sit right inside a sentence without breaking the text flow.

Batman uses a div to park the Batmobile, but a span to hide a Batarang in his belt.
